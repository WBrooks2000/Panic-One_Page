Panic! At the Disco


Brendon Urie

Brendon Urie is an American singer and songwriter who is best known as the lead vocalist and the last original member of "Panic! At The Disco". Panic's first lead vocalist was Ryan ross but shortly after Urie joined "Panic! At The Disco" his vocal talent was recognized and he took over as lead singer.

"I Write Sins Not Tragedies"


"I Write sins not Tradgedies" was released in 2006 and reached #7 on the united states billboard hot 100 their only top 40 hit until 2015

"Nine In The Afternoon"


"Nine In The Afternoon" was released in 2008 and was Panic! at the disco's first song that didn't contain the exclamation point!

"Let's Kill Tonight"

"Let's Kill Tonight" was released in 2011. Brendon Urie says that "The lyrics kind of talk about just the idea of, you know, when you’re like feeling cocky and you’re out with your friends, and you’re like "Yeah! We’re gonna kill it tonight, this is gonna be awesome”, and that's really what it was about, just, you know, one of those good times, the party times."

"Death of a Bachelor"


"Death Of A Bachelor" was released in 2016 and debuted at numbver one on the US Billboard 200.


"High Hopes"


"High Hopes" was released in 2018 and reached the number 4 spot on the Billboard Top 100.
